The Game of Go: Culture & History

Go is a type of strategic board game played with a gridded board and black and white stones. It originated in China and was known by various names such as "yi," "qi," and "shuotan" in ancient China, constituting one of the Four Arts alongside music, chess, and calligraphy.

The Chinese Chess Academy

On March 15, 1992, the inauguration ceremony of the Chinese Chess Academy was held with great solemnity. Construction of the Chinese Chess Academy began in October 1988 and lasted for over three years before its completion. The Chinese Chess Academy is a comprehensive training base and competition venue for chess sports in China. It operates directly under the National Sports Commission and is responsible for managing the national Go, Chinese chess, and international chess teams' training tasks. Additionally, it oversees various major domestic and international competitions for these three types of chess. Furthermore, the academy organizes a wide range of recreational chess activities for the general public.

In November 2019, the Chess and Card Sports Management Center of the General Administration of Sport of China (China Chess Institute) was designated as the guardian unit for the national-level intangible cultural heritage representative project "Chinese Chess."

The China Weiqi League A

In its inaugural year, the Chinese Weiqi A League featured only ten teams. The competition followed a home-and-away format with 18 matches played over two rounds. In subsequent editions, each season of the league comprised twelve teams, with a total of 22 matches played over two rounds. Each club could field four out of six registered team members for participation. The teams were not restricted to players affiliated with the Chinese Chess Institute, leading to the presence of numerous Korean and Japanese players, including foreign recruits, in both the A and B Leagues.

Starting from 2006, there is a championship match between the champion of the Chinese Weiqi A League and the champion of the Korean league.
