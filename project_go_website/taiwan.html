The Game of Go: Culture & History

Go is a type of strategic board game played with a gridded board and black and white stones. It originated in China and was known by various names such as "yi," "qi," and "shuotan" in ancient China, constituting one of the Four Arts alongside music, chess, and calligraphy.

The Haifong Go Academy

Mr. Lin Wenbo, in the year 1997, due to his passion for Go, decided to donate thirty million NT dollars to establish a foundation with the aim of promoting Go. After consulting with professional Go player Lin Haifeng and receiving his full support, Mr. Lin agreed to serve as the first chairman of the foundation. Thus, the "Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation" was established, embarking on the mission of promoting Go. As organizing both professional and amateur competitions requires a significant amount of funds and manpower, the Haifeng Foundation has long been supported financially by Chairman Lin Wenbo. Despite growing rapidly, there was still a perceived shortage in organizing professional competitions, prompting the need for organizational adjustments. Chairman Lin decided to merge the "Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation" into the "Peisen Cultural and Educational Foundation" to maximize efficiency.

The Peisen Cultural and Educational Foundation, another charitable foundation supported by Chairman Lin Wenbo and his family, has been engaged in special education work. Due to its ample financial resources compared to the Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation, the decision was made to merge the two entities. Following the merger, the Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation gracefully stepped down, passing the baton of promoting Go to the Peisen Cultural and Educational Foundation. In response to the needs of Go promotion, the staff of the former Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation underwent adjustments and reorganized into the "Haifeng Go Academy," continuing to engage in the forefront of promoting Go.
